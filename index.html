About Dr. Hariparsad

Dr. Rikesh Dhuneshwar Hariparsad is a distinguished orthopaedic surgeon with over 20 years of experience in musculoskeletal medicine. Born and raised in KwaZulu-Natal, he has dedicated his career to serving communities across South Africa while also gaining valuable international experience in the United Kingdom.

Professional Journey

After completing his medical degree at the prestigious University of KwaZulu-Natal in 2000, Dr. Hariparsad pursued specialised training in anaesthetics, earning his Diploma in Anaesthetics (DA) in 2004. This unique background provides him with exceptional insight into pain management techniques that benefit his orthopaedic patients.

His passion for musculoskeletal medicine led him to specialise in orthopaedics, where he completed his Higher Diploma in Orthopaedics (H. Dip Orth) in 2011, followed by a Fellowship in Orthopaedics in 2012 from the Colleges of Medicine of South Africa.

International and National Experience

Dr. Hariparsad has practiced extensively throughout South Africa, serving diverse communities in both urban and rural settings. His international experience includes working in the United Kingdom's National Health Service (NHS), where he gained exposure to different healthcare systems and advanced surgical techniques.

This breadth of experience has not only enhanced his professional expertise but has also enriched his personal understanding of different cultures and healthcare approaches. He brings this global perspective to his practice while maintaining a deep commitment to serving the people of KwaZulu-Natal.

Patient-Centred Philosophy

Dr. Hariparsad believes in a holistic approach to orthopaedic care, combining evidence-based medicine with personalised treatment plans informed by his extensive experience. He takes time to educate patients about their conditions and involves them in decision-making about their care.

Recovery times vary depending on the type of joint replacement (hip, knee, or shoulder) and the patient's overall health. Most patients can resume light activities within a couple of weeks, while full recovery, including strength and mobility, may take a couple of months. Physical therapy is crucial for optimal results, and Dr. Hariparsad will provide a personalised rehabilitation plan.

Many conditions can be managed without surgery. Treatment options include:

- Physical therapy (strengthening and mobility exercises)
- Medications (anti-inflammatories or pain relief)
- Injections (corticosteroids or hyaluronic acid for lubrication)
- Lifestyle modifications (weight management, activity adjustments)

Dr. Hariparsad will recommend the best approach based on your diagnosis.

Surgery is typically considered when:

- Non-surgical treatments fail to relieve pain/instability
- The injury significantly impairs daily function (e.g., fractures, torn ligaments)
- Imaging (X-rays/MRIs) shows severe damage (e.g., advanced arthritis)

Dr. Hariparsad will conduct a thorough assessment and discuss all options with you before recommending surgery.

Your first visit will include:

1. Medical history review (previous injuries, surgeries, medications)
2. Physical examination (range of motion, strength tests)
3. Diagnostic imaging (if needed: X-rays, MRI, or ultrasound)
4. Personalised treatment plan (surgical or non-surgical)

Bring any prior scans, reports, and a list of questions to make the most of your appointment.
